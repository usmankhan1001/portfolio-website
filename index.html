Hi, I'm Usman Khan

I studied Mechanical Engineering, because I figured building things that exist in the real world was marginally more satisfying than just staring at equations. Since then, I've been somewhere between CAD models, finite element meshes, and real machines that either vibrate too much or not enough.

My work usually revolves around structural simulations, vibration analysis, and designing components that (ideally) don’t fail under load. I use SolidWorks and Fusion 360 not just to make 3D models, but to argue with manufacturing realities. Whether it’s fixing stress concentrations or designing a fixture that won’t require five extra hands to assemble — that’s where I’m usually found.

Currently, I’m also pursuing a Master’s degree in Robotics and AI — because apparently, mechanical parts aren’t allowed to just sit still anymore. They now have to think, see, and sometimes ignore commands entirely. So I’m learning how to make things that move smarter, not just smoother.

Lately, I've been working in axial flux motor development at an EV startup, where we try to build compact machines that don’t explode under torque. Slowly learning to speak the language of electrons too, mostly out of necessity.
